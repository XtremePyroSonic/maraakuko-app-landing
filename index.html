What it is

One feed. Every side of the story.

News apps optimise for speed. Mara Akụkọ optimises for understanding — what happened, who’s saying so, and how much the sources agree.

Questions

Asked and answered.

Where can I get Mara Akụkọ?

Mara Akụkọ is live now. Download it free on the App Store for iPhone and iPad, or on Google Play for Android.

How much does it cost?

The core app is free — the feed, the Daily Briefing with Perspectives, trust badges, creator content, and translation. Premium adds Daily Insight, Trust Heatmaps, Source Balance, and Smart Alerts as a monthly or annual subscription with a free trial, billed through the App Store or Google Play. Cancel anytime.

What does the name mean?

Mara Akụkọ is Igbo for “know the story”. A griot is West Africa’s storyteller, historian, and keeper of memory — the app is built to give that role modern tools.

How are trust signals decided?

Stories are checked across sources for fact agreement and graded into consensus tiers — Superior, Strong, Fair, or Weak. AI models help with evaluation, but they are advisory only: humans make every enforcement decision, and each AI evaluation records a confidence level and rationale. The full methodology is public in our AI Transparency policy.

Which languages are supported?

The interface ships in 15 languages — English, Arabic, Bengali, German, Spanish, French, Hindi, Indonesian, Japanese, Portuguese, Russian, Swahili, Turkish, Urdu, and Chinese. Stories can also be translated automatically, with a per-story toggle to view the original.

How do creators get paid?

Readers can tip any creator’s work directly in the app. Tips are split 80/20 in the creator’s favor, with payouts via Stripe or PayPal under the published Creator Agreement.

What happens with my data?

The Privacy Policy spells out exactly what is collected and why, and you can delete your account and data from inside the app at any time — see Account & Data Deletion. This website itself uses cookieless, aggregate analytics only.

Where is it available?

On iOS and Android through the App Store and Google Play; country availability follows the stores. Coverage centers on Africa and its diaspora — and it’s readable from anywhere.
