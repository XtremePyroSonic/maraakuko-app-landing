Copyright & Takedown Policy

Last updated 2026-06-03

Filing a notice

If you believe content on Mara Akụkọ infringes your copyright, you may file a takedown notice. You can file from within the app or, if you do not have an account, through our public copyright form. A valid notice must identify the work and the infringing material and include the sworn statements required by 17 U.S.C. §512(c)(3): a good-faith belief, accuracy under penalty of perjury, and your authority to act for the rightsholder.

What happens next

Valid notices are reviewed by a human. If a takedown is issued the material is removed and the affected creator is notified.

Counter-notice

If your content was removed and you believe it was a mistake or that you are authorized to use it, you may file a counter-notice. After the statutory waiting period the material may be reinstated unless the original claimant pursues legal action.

Repeat infringers

In accordance with 17 U.S.C. §512(i), accounts that accumulate repeated, validated copyright strikes may be terminated.
